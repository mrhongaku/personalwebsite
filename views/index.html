About Me

I am a second-year intended Economics and Computer Science Major at UC Berkeley.

I am a student, an artist and a teacher.

In high school I discovered my love for hip hop music. I am now on a quest to find, chop, and mix the perfect sample. I mix hip hop elements with different, often completely polar, genres. I use classical elements with hip hop sounds and 808s with Asian instruments. I am heavily influenced by producers such as J. Dilla, Nujabes, Jeftuz, NeguimBeatz, Wayvee, Knxwledge, Mr. Carmack, 9th Wonder, and Black Milk.

Now, I am an Arts and Entertainment reporter for the Daily Californian, focusing on exposing Northern California's ever-growing hip hop culture. I am also involved in Berkeley's Rotaract Club as well as being the Marketing Director of Rotaract District 5160, encompassing a large range of Northern Californian colleges.

I was born and raised in California and will live here forever. This is my home.
